\begin{abstract}
  LIGNUM  is  a   functional-structural  tree  model  that  represents  coniferous and broad-leaved trees  with four modelling units closely  resembling  the  real  structure  of  trees.   The  units  are  tree  segments,  the  tree axes,  branching  points  and buds.   Metabolic  processes are  explicitly related to  the structural units  in which  they are  taking place.  Here we  enhance the model  LIGNUM with the  possibility to formally describe  the architectural development of a  tree with Lindenmayer systems.    We give sample applications of  the system to model single trees and  forest stand.  Finally we discuss our approach  and its consequences  for the future development of the model.
\end{abstract}
